Here's why you should get involved

  • Saving natural resources: Reusing discarded products and using recycled materials to make new products reduces the use of new materials, which often involves harvesting trees and mining the earth. Even better, “source reducing” waste — or avoiding the generation of discards in the first place — saves the most resources of all.
  • Saving energy: Enormous amounts of energy are required to make products and materials worldwide. Energy is saved by not using products and materials when at all possible, or by reusing products in their current form.
  • Preventing pollution: Almost all manufacturing processes use water and release wastewater and air emissions into the environment. Reducing and reusing products cuts down on manufacturing pollution, just as the use of recycled materials prevents pollution in industrial processes like paper production.
  • Saving money: Households can save money by reusing materials and products and by practicing smart shopping habits that reduce waste.
